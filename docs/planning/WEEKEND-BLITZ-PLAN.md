# Weekend Blitz: The Lobster Protocol Campaign

**Created:** 2026-04-10
**Status:** READY TO EXECUTE
**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts (paid)
**Video tool:** Grok Imagine (AI video generation)
**Campaign window:** Saturday-Sunday (2 days, 6 posts)
**Tone:** Confident, slightly mysterious, tech-savvy. Never cheesy, never desperate.

---

## Campaign Strategy

### Story Arc

The six posts follow a classic ARG reveal structure:

```
Post 1 (Sat AM)   — THE SIGNAL      Something is hidden. No context.
Post 2 (Sat NOON) — THE DISCOVERY    Someone found something weird on a website.
Post 3 (Sat PM)   — THE TERMINAL     The hidden terminal is revealed.
Post 4 (Sun AM)   — THE HUNT         Show the puzzle mechanics and community reaction.
Post 5 (Sun PM)   — THE STAKES       Reveal the prizes. Show the leaderboard.
Post 6 (Sun EVE)  — THE CLOCK        Only 5 winners. The race is on.
```

Each post gives slightly more information than the last. The first two should generate "what is this?" comments. The middle two should generate "I need to try this" intent. The final two should generate "I need to do this NOW" action.

### Audience Targeting (Paid)

Primary segments for ad targeting:

- **Resellers/flippers:** Interest in eBay selling, thrift flipping, garage sales, Poshmark, estate sales
- **Tech/puzzle community:** Interest in CTF challenges, escape rooms, coding, ARGs, internet mysteries
- **Deal hunters:** Interest in coupons, bargain shopping, Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace
- **Nostalgia/retro tech:** Interest in retro computing, 90s internet, vaporwave, retrowave

### Creative Direction for Grok Imagine

All videos should feel like they were recorded by someone who stumbled onto something they shouldn't have. Screen recordings mixed with atmospheric AI-generated visuals. Dark backgrounds, green terminal text, CRT scanlines. The aesthetic is "someone recording their screen at 2am because they found something weird."

Do NOT use: bright colors, upbeat music, sales language, product screenshots of the main Flip-ly site (save the product reveal for the terminal experience itself).

DO use: dark ambient music, glitch effects, monospace text, terminal aesthetics, the ASCII lobster art, green-on-black color scheme, CRT monitor filters.

## Production Notes

### Video Specs

| Platform | Aspect Ratio | Resolution | Max Length |
|----------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| TikTok | 9:16 | 1080x1920 | 60s |
| Instagram Reels | 9:16 | 1080x1920 | 90s |
| YouTube Shorts | 9:16 | 1080x1920 | 60s |

### Audio

- Posts 1, 2, 6: Ambient/minimal. Server hum, keyboard clicks, silence.
- Posts 3, 4: Dark electronic. Building tension. Think: Mr. Robot soundtrack energy.
- Post 5: Clean, confident. Minimal beat. Let the text carry the weight.

No voiceover on any post. The text and visuals tell the story. If Grok Imagine doesn't support audio, add it in post-production using royalty-free dark ambient tracks.

### Text Overlays

Use a monospace font (JetBrains Mono or similar) for all text overlays. White or green text on dark backgrounds. No rounded corners, no drop shadows, no gradients. Clean and terminal-like.

### Cross-Post Adjustments

- **TikTok:** Optimize for discoverability. Front-load the hook (first 2 seconds must stop the scroll). Use TikTok-native text overlays in addition to video text.
- **Instagram Reels:** Same video, but add to Stories with a poll: "Would you try to crack this?" Use the countdown sticker for Post 6.
- **YouTube Shorts:** Same video. Add an end screen pointing to a longer "how the Lobster Protocol works" video if one exists.

### Engagement Strategy

- **Reply to every "what is this?" comment** with cryptic but helpful hints. Never fully explain. Example replies: "inspect the source," "the lobster sees all," "5 pixels. bottom right."
- **Pin a comment** on Post 1 after Post 2 goes live: "Update: someone found the terminal. The hunt is on."
- **Do NOT reply with the URL** in comments on TikTok (it gets shadowbanned). Say "check the bio" or just the domain name as text.
- **Repost/stitch** anyone who makes content about finding the terminal or cracking levels. This is the organic amplification layer.
